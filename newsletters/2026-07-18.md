# AI News — Saturday, July 18, 2026

> China owned the day: Moonshot dropped a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight frontier model just as Xi Jinping personally opened the World AI Conference in Shanghai — while Washington fights over the first NVIDIA chips shipped east under the new export licenses.

## Top Stories

### [Moonshot releases Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight frontier model](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/moonshot-releases-2-8-trillion-parameter-kimi-k3)
China's Moonshot AI released Kimi K3 — a 2.8T-parameter mixture-of-experts model (16 of 896 experts active) built on Kimi Delta Attention, with weights slated to go public by July 27. It is the largest open-weight release to date and posts benchmark wins over Western frontier models on coding tasks.

### [Xi Jinping opens the World AI Conference, calling for a global AI effort](https://www.npr.org/2026/07/17/nx-s1-5897285/chinas-xi-calls-for-step-up-of-global-effort-in-ai-as-us-curbs-squeeze-chinas-tech-access)
China's president delivered the opening keynote of the 2026 World AI Conference in Shanghai — his first appearance in the event's history — urging a stepped-up global AI effort as US export curbs squeeze China's access to advanced chips. The speech underscores how central AI has become to US–China strategic competition.

### [First NVIDIA H200 chips ship to China under new US licenses](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/small-amount-of-nvidia-ai-chips-shipped-to-china-with-us-license)
A "trivial" number of H200 processors reached Chinese customers after US approval, the Commerce Department said, the first shipments under the loosened export rules. The move has sparked a bipartisan fight in Washington over enforcement, while the newest Blackwell-class chips remain license-restricted.
