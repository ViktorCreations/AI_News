# AI News — Monday, July 20, 2026

> Governance and security set the tone: Shanghai's World AI Conference closes with China positioning itself as convener of global AI rules, Brussels pries Android open for rival assistants — and Hugging Face discloses a production breach executed end-to-end by an autonomous AI agent.

## Top Stories

### [Hugging Face discloses a breach carried out by an autonomous AI agent](https://www.thestack.technology/hugging-face-hacked-turned-to-chinese-llm-for-help-after-us-models-blocked-blue-team/)
In a July 16 incident report, Hugging Face said an autonomous agent system exploited code-execution flaws in dataset processing, harvested credentials, and moved laterally across internal clusters — leaving 17,000+ logged attacker actions. A striking detail: US commercial models refused to analyze the real exploit payloads on safety grounds, so the defense team pivoted to the open-weight Chinese model GLM 5.2 running on Hugging Face's own infrastructure.

### [WAIC 2026 closes in Shanghai after a record four days](https://english.news.cn/20260717/ce32e833ab5d47f883ad44e1f73cb634/c.html)
The World AI Conference wraps today after a record-scale four days in which Xi Jinping's first-ever WAIC keynote called for "equitable" global AI governance amid US export controls. Beyond the new 29-country WAICO body, China pledged 5,000 AI training opportunities for developing countries over five years, an "AI Capacity-Building Action Plan," and access for 30 countries to its MAZU AI meteorological warning system.

### [EU orders Google to open Android to rival AI assistants and share search data](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/commission-provides-guidance-google-ai-interoperability-android-and-sharing-google-search-data-under-2026-07-16_en)
In binding Digital Markets Act measures issued last Thursday, the European Commission required Google to give competing AI assistants equal access to Android features — including voice activation comparable to "Hey Google" — and to share anonymized search data with rival engines and AI chatbots under a regulated pricing formula. It is the most direct regulatory intervention yet in the AI assistant market.

### [Alibaba previews Qwen3.8-Max, claiming 2.4 trillion parameters](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/19/alibaba-previews-qwen3-8-max-a-2-4-trillion-parameter-multimodal-model-days-after-moonshots-kimi-k3-open-weight-launch/)
Two days after Moonshot's Kimi K3, Alibaba used WAIC to preview its next flagship: a sparse mixture-of-experts multimodal model the company says ranks second only to Claude Fable 5 — though no benchmark table has been published and the parameter count is unverified. The preview is live at discounted pricing, with open weights promised "soon" but no date or license yet.
