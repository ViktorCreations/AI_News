# AI News — Wednesday, July 22, 2026

> Decoupling ran both directions today: Beijing weighed export curbs on its own AI models as Z.ai powered up a gigawatt data center with zero Nvidia silicon — while the two governments quietly planned September AI talks. Meanwhile Google shipped new Flash models (still no Pro), and the largest copyright settlement in US history was sealed.

## Top Stories

### [Google ships Gemini 3.6 Flash and 3.5 Flash-Lite — the delayed 3.5 Pro remains absent](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/changelog)
Google's API changelog shows both models reaching general availability July 21: Gemini 3.6 Flash brings better token efficiency and code/agentic planning at a lower price than 3.5 Flash, while Flash-Lite targets low-latency, high-volume subagent work. Notably missing is Gemini 3.5 Pro, the flagship whose months-long delay wiped ~$200B off Alphabet's market value when Bloomberg reported it last week.

### [Anthropic's $1.5B copyright settlement wins final court approval](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/20/anthropics-landmark-1-5b-copyright-settlement-is-approved/)
Judge Araceli Martinez-Olguin signed off Monday on the largest settlement in US copyright history: roughly $3,000 per work across ~500,000 books that Anthropic downloaded from pirate libraries to train Claude. The class action ends without trial — and without binding precedent for the copyright suits still pending against other AI companies.

## Compute & Data Centers

- **[Z.ai powers up a 1GW-class AI data center built entirely on Chinese chips](https://technode.com/2026/07/21/z-ai-completes-construction-of-a-1gw-class-ai-data-center-using-chinese-chips/)** — the GLM developer's facility is partially operating with multiple 10,000+-chip clusters and no Nvidia silicon, a milestone for Beijing's push to train frontier models on domestic hardware (first reported by Bloomberg).

## Regulation & Policy

- **[The US and China plan formal AI talks in September](https://thenextweb.com/news/us-china-ai-talks-september)** — the channel, building on commitments from Trump's May visit to Beijing, is expected to cover AI safety and governance with chip export controls looming over the agenda; neither capital has confirmed a date.
- **[China is weighing export controls on its own AI models and chips](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/china-considers-tighter-export-controls-041139427.html)** — per an FT report, the Commerce Ministry has consulted Alibaba, ByteDance, and Zhipu on restricting overseas transfer of training data, foreign downloads of model weights, and advanced chip designs in the next revision of its export catalogue.
- **[AI labs set US lobbying records in Q2](https://issueone.org/press/big-tech-spends-millions-to-buy-influence-in-washington-in-first-half-of-2026/)** — Anthropic spent a record $1.97M (topping Nvidia's $1.25M), OpenAI $1.2M; four years ago none of the three had federal lobbyists.
