# AI News — Sunday, July 19, 2026

> The weekend's through-line is compute economics: Anthropic is reportedly negotiating for Meta's spare GPUs, Apple briefly passed Nvidia as investors rethink who profits from the AI buildout — and in Shanghai, China turned the World AI Conference into a showcase of homegrown chips and humanoid robots.

## Top Stories

### [Anthropic in talks to lease up to $10B of Meta computing power](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/anthropic-talks-lease-meta-computing-170931284.html)
Anthropic is in very preliminary talks to lease AI computing capacity from Meta in a deal that could reach $10 billion over two years, first reported by The New York Times. Nothing is signed and either side could walk, but the talks would turn Meta's massive data-center buildout into a rentable cloud business — putting it in competition with Amazon, Microsoft, and Google.

### [Apple briefly overtakes Nvidia as the world's most valuable company](https://www.foxbusiness.com/markets/apple-briefly-overtakes-nvidia-worlds-most-valuable-company-amid-ai-investment-doubts)
Apple passed Nvidia in intraday trading Friday, topping $4.91 trillion in market value as investors reassess AI winners: analysts note Apple is less exposed to capital-intensive data-center spending and better positioned to monetize AI through services. Nvidia closed back on top at $4.92 trillion to Apple's $4.89 trillion, underlining how tight the race has become.

### [GPT-5.6 produces a proof closing a 30-year gap in convex optimization](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48957779)
The weekend's most-discussed AI story (567 points on Hacker News): a researcher reports GPT-5.6 proved that optimizing convex Lipschitz functions requires Ω(d²) function evaluations — matching a 30-year-old algorithm and closing the gap between upper and lower bounds. Caveats apply: the result is not peer-reviewed, and commenters note the 10-page prompt encoded much of the proof technique, making this as much a story about AI-assisted mathematics as autonomous discovery.

## Research & Models

- **[SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23904)** — the #1 trending paper on Hugging Face this weekend (257 upvotes): a text-space optimizer that edits an agent's skill documents from scored rollouts, adding up to 23.5 accuracy points across benchmarks without touching model weights.
