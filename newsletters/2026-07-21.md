# AI News — Tuesday, July 21, 2026

> The buildout hit its limits and its next gear on the same day: Moonshot ran out of GPUs for its hit open-weight model, Hut 8 fully leased a gigawatt AI campus, Bristol Myers bought pharma's biggest AI supercomputer — and Washington is weighing a FINRA-style referee for frontier models.

## Top Stories

### [Moonshot pauses new Kimi K3 subscriptions after demand maxes out its GPUs](https://the-decoder.com/moonshot-pauses-new-kimi-k3-subscriptions-after-gpu-demand-maxes-out-in-48-hours/)
Four days after the launch covered in our July 18 issue, Moonshot said demand for Kimi K3 "pushed close to the limits of our current capacity" within 48 hours and temporarily stopped taking new subscribers — existing users are unaffected, and slots will reopen in batches. The company is also splitting its offering into separate Kimi and Kimi Code memberships to spread compute more evenly, a vivid data point on how compute-constrained China's frontier labs remain.

### [CuspAI raises $450M and launches an "AI Materials Foundry" with Nvidia and Meta](https://thenextweb.com/news/cuspai-ai-materials-foundry-coalition-450m)
The materials-discovery startup's Series B — led by Kleiner Perkins and NEA with backing from Jeff Bezos's fund — values it at $2.6 billion and funds a coalition of 45+ companies and labs (Nvidia, Meta, Samsung, Applied Materials, Lam Research among them) pooling compute and lab access. An early target: designing out the rare metals like iridium and ruthenium that chipmaking depends on.

### [Bristol Myers Squibb is deploying an Nvidia Vera Rubin SuperPOD for drug discovery](https://finance.yahoo.com/healthcare/articles/bristol-myers-squibb-expands-nvidia-123726898.html)
The drugmaker is expanding its three-year Nvidia partnership with a DGX SuperPOD built on Vera Rubin NVL72 systems — an architecture Nvidia says delivers up to ten times more performance per megawatt than the prior generation — to power foundation-model training and agentic workflows across oncology, immunology, and neuroscience research.

### [US officials weigh a FINRA-like watchdog to vet frontier AI models](https://www.business-standard.com/world-news/us-considers-creating-finra-like-watchdog-to-vet-advanced-ai-models-126071800117_1.html)
A proposal reported last week — developed with input from Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent — would create an industry-funded self-regulatory body under SEC oversight to review advanced models before deployment. Google DeepMind's Demis Hassabis published a similar framework that drew public support from Sam Altman and Elon Musk; the plan remains under White House review and could change.
