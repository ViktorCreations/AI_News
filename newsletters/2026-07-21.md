# AI News — Tuesday, July 21, 2026

> The buildout hit its limits and its next gear on the same day: Moonshot ran out of GPUs for its hit open-weight model, Hut 8 fully leased a gigawatt AI campus, Bristol Myers bought pharma's biggest AI supercomputer — and Washington is weighing a FINRA-style referee for frontier models.

## Top Stories

### [Moonshot pauses new Kimi K3 subscriptions after demand maxes out its GPUs](https://the-decoder.com/moonshot-pauses-new-kimi-k3-subscriptions-after-gpu-demand-maxes-out-in-48-hours/)
Four days after the launch covered in our July 18 issue, Moonshot said demand for Kimi K3 "pushed close to the limits of our current capacity" within 48 hours and temporarily stopped taking new subscribers — existing users are unaffected, and slots will reopen in batches. The company is also splitting its offering into separate Kimi and Kimi Code memberships to spread compute more evenly, a vivid data point on how compute-constrained China's frontier labs remain.

## Research & Models

- **[Anthropic opens AI for Science grants for rare disease research](https://www.anthropic.com/news/rare-disease-research-grants)** — up to $50,000 in Claude credits over six months per team, across basic-science and biotech tracks, in partnership with the Monarch Initiative; applications close August 2.

## Compute & Data Centers

- **[Bristol Myers Squibb is deploying an Nvidia Vera Rubin SuperPOD for drug discovery](https://finance.yahoo.com/healthcare/articles/bristol-myers-squibb-expands-nvidia-123726898.html)** — the drugmaker expands its three-year Nvidia partnership with DGX Vera Rubin NVL72 systems (up to 10x more performance per megawatt, per Nvidia) for foundation-model training and agentic research workflows across oncology, immunology, and neuroscience.

## Regulation & Policy

- **[US officials weigh a FINRA-like watchdog to vet frontier AI models](https://www.business-standard.com/world-news/us-considers-creating-finra-like-watchdog-to-vet-advanced-ai-models-126071800117_1.html)** — the proposal reported last week, developed with input from Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, would create an industry-funded self-regulatory body under SEC oversight to review models before deployment; Demis Hassabis's similar framework drew support from Sam Altman and Elon Musk, and the plan remains under White House review.

## Capital & Deals

- **[CuspAI raises $450M at a $2.6B valuation, launching an "AI Materials Foundry" with Nvidia and Meta](https://thenextweb.com/news/cuspai-ai-materials-foundry-coalition-450m)** — the Kleiner Perkins/NEA-led Series B, backed by Jeff Bezos's fund, funds a 45+-member coalition pooling compute and labs to design out chipmaking's rare metals.
- **[Tempus AI to acquire cancer-test maker Personalis for ~$1.5B](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/tempus-to-acquire-cancer-test-maker-personalis-for-15b/825698/)** — the mostly-stock deal at $16.25/share folds Personalis's tumor-informed MRD testing into Tempus's AI oncology platform; expected to close late 2026 or early 2027.
- **[Databricks is raising at a $188B valuation](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-raising-strategic-round-funding-188-billion-valuation)** — announced last week, the Coatue-led strategic round funds its agent and database products as CEO Ali Ghodsi pitches enterprises moving from "tokenmaxxing to valuemaxxing."
- **[Hut 8 signs a second $9.8B AI data-center lease, fully commercializing its Texas campus](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/hut-8-signs-9-8-123632954.html)** — the 15-year, 352 MW deal doubles the unnamed investment-grade tenant's footprint to 704 MW and takes the former bitcoin miner's contracted AI portfolio to 949 MW and $26.6B in base contract value.
- **[Etched is reportedly seeking a ~$20B valuation in back-to-back rounds](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/etched-seeks-20-billion-valuation-in-new-ai-chip-funding-round--wsj-reports-4799566)** — per WSJ, Jane Street would lead at $20B alongside a separate Sequoia round at $10B for the inference-chip maker; neither deal has closed.
